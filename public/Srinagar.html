About Hazratbal Shrine

Hazratbal Shrine, located in Srinagar, Jammu & Kashmir, is one of the holiest places in Kashmir. It is known for preserving a hair strand of Prophet Muhammad, which is referred to as Moi-e-Muqaddas or 'the relic.' The word "Hazrat" means holy or respected, and "Bal" stands for place, making it a 'holy place' for Muslims and a significant site for tourists visiting Srinagar.

The relic is displayed to the public on certain occasions such as the birthday of Prophet Muhammad and his four main companions: Hazrat Abu Bakr Siddique, Hazrat Umar ibn Khattab, Hazrat Usman ibn Affan, and Hazrat Ali. These events draw devotees from across the country who come to pay their respects.

History of Hazratbal Shrine

The relic was brought to India by Syed Abdullah, believed to be a descendant of Prophet Muhammad. His son, Syed Hamid, inherited the relic but was unable to care for it. He sold it to a wealthy Kashmiri businessman, Khwaja Nur-ud-din Eshai, during the reign of Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb. Upon hearing about the relic, Aurangzeb ordered its seizure and imprisoned Khwaja Nur-ud-din Eshai.

In 1700, the relic was returned to Kashmir with the deceased body of Khwaja Nur-ud-din Eshai, who died in imprisonment. His daughter, Inayat Begum, became the official custodian of the relic and established the shrine. Today, the Banday Family has taken over the responsibility of maintaining the shrine.

Architecture of Hazratbal Shrine

Hazratbal Shrine is an architectural marvel that blends both Mughal and Kashmiri styles. The dome and minaret are heavily influenced by Mughal architecture, while the intricate details and design elements reflect Kashmiri traditions. Its serene location on the northern banks of Dal Lake adds to its beauty, making it one of the most visited places in Srinagar.

Things to Know Before Visiting Hazratbal Shrine

Best Time to Visit

If you wish to witness the relic of Hazratbal Shrine, it is best to plan your visit on special occasions like the birthday of Prophet Muhammad or that of his companions. During these times, the hair of Prophet Muhammad is displayed in a glass casket for the public.

Things to Keep in Mind

When visiting the shrine, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Fridays are very crowded due to Jumma Namaz (Friday prayers), so it's advised to avoid visiting on this day.
  • Women are only allowed to enter the first part of the mosque.

Visiting Hazratbal Shrine and Other Attractions in Srinagar

Apart from Hazratbal Shrine, Srinagar offers many other attractions, such as the famous Dal Lake, Shankaracharya Temple, Mughal Gardens, and the Floating Vegetable Market. A Shikara ride on Dal Lake is a unique experience that allows you to witness the beauty of Srinagar.

Embrace the heavenly beauty of Srinagar, the crown jewel of Kashmir, with its serene lakes and stunning landscapes!
