About the Temple

The Meenakshi Amman Temple in Madurai, often referred to as the 'Athens of the East,' is a sacred place that offers solace to those overwhelmed by worldly affairs. Located on the southern bank of the River Vaigai in Tamil Nadu, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Parvati, known as 'Meenakshi,' and her consort Lord Shiva, referred to as 'Sundareswarar.' The temple forms the geographical and cultural heart of Madurai, aptly nicknamed the 'Temple City.'

Architectural Marvel

The temple showcases magnificent architectural elements, including:

  • 12 gateway towers (Gopurams)
  • 2 golden shrines (Viamanas) over the sanctum of the main deities
  • A temple tank with a golden lotus
  • Halls, including the one with a thousand intricately carved pillars
  • Musical pillars and mandapam

The rituals, worship, and festivals associated with the temple attract devotees and tourists from around the globe.

Historical Significance

According to legend, the temple originated when the King of Devas, Indra, discovered the sacred 'Suyambu Lingam' (a form of Lord Shiva) at Kadambavanam and enshrined it at Madurai. The current structure was built by Sadayavarman Sundara Pandyan in the 12th century, with further extensions made by the Nayaka rulers between the 12th and 18th centuries.

One captivating legend tells of Goddess Parvati, born with three breasts to King Malayadwaja Pandya and Queen Kanchanamalai as a divine blessing. Her third breast disappeared when she met Lord Shiva, fulfilling her destiny. Their celestial union is celebrated through the presiding deities of the temple.

Cultural Activities

The temple is a vibrant center for cultural and religious activities. Visitors can witness rituals, participate in worship, and enjoy grand festivals that celebrate the divine love and union of Meenakshi and Sundareswarar. The annual Meenakshi Thirukalyanam (celestial wedding) festival is a major highlight.

Travel Information

Best Time to Visit

The temple is open to visitors year-round.

Getting There

  • By Air: Madurai Airport, located 12 km from the city, offers domestic and limited international flights.
  • By Rail: Direct trains connect Madurai to major cities like Bangalore, Chennai, Mumbai, and Delhi.
  • By Road: Madurai is well-connected by road, with three major bus terminals facilitating interstate and intrastate travel.
