About Basilica of Bom Jesus

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is one of the most iconic churches in Goa and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is renowned for housing the relics of St. Francis Xavier, a missionary saint who played a major role in the spread of Christianity in Asia. The basilica, a blend of architectural and artistic grandeur, is a true testament to Goa’s rich colonial past.

The mausoleum of St. Francis Xavier, a masterpiece crafted by Giovanni Batista Foggini, stands within the basilica and is a central attraction for visitors from around the world.

History of Basilica of Bom Jesus

The foundation stone of the Basilica of Bom Jesus was laid on November 24, 1594, and its construction was completed on May 16, 1605. The church was elevated to the status of a minor basilica in 1946. The basilica houses the relics of St. Francis Xavier, and the process of enshrining his relics was completed on April 24, 1659.

The church has witnessed numerous historical events and is a symbol of the Portuguese influence in Goa, reflecting the rich cultural blend of colonial architecture and Goan heritage.

Architecture of Basilica of Bom Jesus

The architecture of the Basilica of Bom Jesus is a classic example of Baroque style, with influences from Roman, Doric, Ionic, Corinthian, and Composite styles. The façade is made of granite and features intricate carvings and a quadrangular pediment at the top.

The basilica is a three-story structure, and its unplastered exterior adds to its rustic charm. The church is renowned for its Baroque architecture and its finely detailed carvings that adorn the main altar and reredos.

Major Highlights of Basilica of Bom Jesus

  • Quadrangular pediment at the top of the façade
  • Carved columns and intricate sculptures
  • Statue of St. Francis Xavier prominently displayed
  • A modern art gallery with Biblical paintings
  • Sacristy containing the Golden Rose
  • The richly carved and gilded main altar
  • Chapel of St. Francis Xavier, displaying paintings of the saint
  • Chapel of the Blessed Sacrament

Visiting Basilica of Bom Jesus

Visiting Time

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is open to visitors every day of the week. The visiting hours are as follows:

  • Monday to Saturday: 9:00 AM to 6:30 PM
  • Sunday: 10:30 AM to 6:30 PM

How to Reach Basilica of Bom Jesus

The Basilica of Bom Jesus is located in Old Goa, approximately 11 km from Panaji. It is easily accessible by taxi or auto-rickshaw from Panaji. The church is situated on the Ponte de Linhares Causeway, making it a convenient stop for tourists exploring Old Goa.

You can also reach the basilica from other parts of Goa by local transport, including buses and taxis.
