About Piran Kaliyar Sharif

Piran Kaliyar Sharif, located south of Haridwar, is believed to possess mystical powers that fulfill the wishes of devotees. The shrine is dedicated to the 13th-century Sufi saint, Alauddin Ali Ahmed Sabir Kalyari, who was a prominent figure in the Chishti order and the successor to Baba Farid. Built by Afghan ruler Ibrahim Lodhi, the shrine symbolizes religious unity and reverence for both Hindus and Muslims.

History of Alauddin Sabir Kalyari

Born in Kohtwaal, a town in Multan, Alauddin Sabir Kalyari was the elder nephew of Baba Farid. He lived a life of asceticism and was known for surviving on what he found in the jungle, earning the title "Sabir." After being invited to Kaliyar Sharif in 1253 AD, he spent his life there, passing away in 1291. His shrine has since become a popular pilgrimage site.

Religious and Cultural Significance

The shrine at Piran Kaliyar Sharif holds immense cultural and religious significance. It attracts pilgrims from all faiths, particularly for its annual Urs Festival, which commemorates the death anniversary of the saint. The shrine continues to be a symbol of unity and reverence.

Cultural Activities at Piran Kaliyar Sharif

The Urs Festival

The Urs Festival is a grand annual event held to commemorate the death anniversary of Alauddin Sabir Kalyari. It is celebrated with fervor, attracting thousands of devotees who gather for prayers, offerings, and celebrations.

Spiritual Activities

Visitors to the shrine often engage in spiritual practices such as zikr (remembrance of God), prayer, and meditation. The shrine provides a serene atmosphere for introspection and devotion, where devotees seek blessings for various aspects of their lives.

How to Reach Piran Kaliyar Sharif

By Road

Piran Kaliyar Sharif is accessible by road from Roorkee, which is well-connected to various regions of North India. Taxis, buses, and rickshaws are readily available from Roorkee to the shrine.

By Rail

Roorkee Railway Station, located just 10 km away, is the nearest railhead. Taxis and private vehicles are available from the station to the shrine.

By Air

The nearest airport to Piran Kaliyar Sharif is Jolly Grant Airport, approximately 61 km away. From there, taxis or private vehicles can be hired to reach the shrine.
