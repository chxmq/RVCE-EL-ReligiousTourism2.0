About Dallina Vardamana Mahaveera Jain Temple

The Dallina Vardamana Mahaveera Jain Temple, located in Sankighatta, Karnataka, is one of the most significant Jain temples in the state. This temple is dedicated to the 24th Tirthankara, Lord Mahavir Bhagwan, and holds deep religious and historical significance for followers of Jainism. Sankighatta, known for its rich Jain heritage, is home to many other temples and pilgrimage sites.

History of the Temple

The origins of this temple date back to the 11th century, during the reign of Emperor Narasimha I of the Hoysala Dynasty. The emperor built this temple as a place of worship for Jain followers. The Settru family, the original patrons of the temple, took over its management and have preserved its traditions for generations. In 1950, the care of the temple was transferred to the Tahsildar of Magadi Taluk, but the Settru family's legacy continues.

Architecture of the Temple

The temple is home to a unique statue of Lord Mahavir, made of panchaloha (five metals). This idol is notable for depicting Mahavir as a young boy with a smiling face, exuding warmth and tranquility. The idol was originally carved from black stone, but over time, it was replaced with the current metal idol due to damage. However, the original black stone idol is still present behind the new metal one, adding to the temple's historical and spiritual charm.

Festivals Celebrated at the Temple

The most significant festival at the temple begins on Diwali day and lasts for an entire month. This festival coincides with Karthika Amavasi, an auspicious day in Hinduism. It marks the day of Mahavir Swamy Moksha Kalyana, the liberation of Lord Mahavir. The temple is beautifully decorated, and devotees from far and wide participate in prayers, rituals, and cultural activities.

Best Time to Visit

The temple attracts visitors year-round, but the Diwali festival, which starts on Karthika Amavasi, is the most popular time to visit. The temple is especially vibrant during this period, with a significant number of pilgrims gathering to partake in the spiritual celebrations and rituals.

How to Reach Sankighatta

By Air

The nearest airport to Sankighatta is located in Bengaluru, about 80 kilometers away. Bengaluru International Airport is well-connected to major cities across India and internationally. From Bengaluru, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Sankighatta.

By Train

Sankighatta is connected to nearby cities like Bengaluru by rail. The nearest railway station is Magadi Junction, about 20 kilometers away from the temple. From there, you can take a taxi or local transport to reach the temple.

By Road

Sankighatta is well-connected by road. Buses and taxis operate from Bengaluru, which is the nearest major city. The drive from Bengaluru to Sankighatta is scenic and pleasant, making it a wonderful journey for pilgrims and tourists alike.
