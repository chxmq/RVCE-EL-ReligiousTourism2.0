About Arthunkal Church

The Arthunkal Church, also known as St. Andrew's Forane Church, is one of the most famous Christian pilgrim centers in Kerala. Located in the scenic coastal town of Arthunkal in the Alappuzha district, this church has been a spiritual beacon for devotees for centuries. Built in the 16th century by Portuguese missionaries, it stands as a testament to Keralaâ€™s rich Christian heritage.

The church attracts visitors from all around the world, especially during the annual festival known as Arthunkal Perunnal, which takes place in January. This event is one of the most significant Christian festivals in Kerala and draws thousands of devotees for its grand celebrations.

Historical Significance of Arthunkal Church

The church was originally built by Portuguese missionaries in the 16th century, marking the spread of Christianity in the region. Over the centuries, the church has become not only a religious center but also an important historical monument that reflects the cultural and spiritual evolution of the region.

The church is dedicated to St. Sebastian, who is believed to have performed numerous miracles during his lifetime. St. Sebastian's statue is prominently featured in the church and plays a central role in the cultural and religious activities that take place there.

Arthunkal Perunnal Festival

The Arthunkal Perunnal festival is the most significant event associated with Arthunkal Church. Held every year in January, this festival attracts thousands of devotees who come to take part in the feast, prayers, and ceremonial processions.

Cultural Activities During the Festival

One of the most unique aspects of Arthunkal Perunnal is the ceremonial procession where the statue of St. Sebastian is taken from the church to the beach and back. Devotees actively participate in this procession, making it a colorful and spiritually charged event.

On the final day of the festival, a unique ritual takes place where devotees crawl on their knees from the nearby beach to the church as an act of devotion and penance. This act symbolizes deep respect for the saint and is believed to bring blessings to the devotees.

Visiting Arthunkal Church

Location

Arthunkal is a beautiful coastal town located about 43 kilometers away from Cochin City and around 21 kilometers from Alappuzha town. The church is easily accessible from both cities, making it a perfect destination for both spiritual and cultural exploration.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Arthunkal Church is during the Arthunkal Perunnal festival, held in January. However, the church and its surrounding area are peaceful and beautiful to visit at any time of the year.

How to Reach Arthunkal

Arthunkal can be easily reached by road, rail, and air:

  • By Road: The town is well-connected by road from Cochin and Alappuzha. You can hire a taxi or take a local bus to reach the church.
  • By Rail: The nearest railway station is Alappuzha, which is 21 kilometers away from the church. From the station, you can hire a taxi or use local transport.
  • By Air: Cochin International Airport is the nearest airport, about 43 kilometers away. From the airport, taxis are available to reach Arthunkal.
