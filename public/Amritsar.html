About Golden Temple

The Golden Temple, also known as Shri Harmandir Sahib, is one of the holiest and most iconic places of worship in India. Located in the city of Amritsar, this stunning temple is surrounded by a serene lake with crystal-clear waters, reflecting its golden structure. The temple is covered in over 750 kg of pure gold, giving it a radiant glow, which makes it a popular destination for pilgrims and tourists alike.

Erected in 1574 by Guru Arjan, the fifth Sikh Guru, it has since become a symbol of peace, harmony, and spiritual solace. Visitors from all walks of life come to this sacred place to experience its tranquil atmosphere and to engage in prayers and meditation.

Historical Significance of Golden Temple

The history of Golden Temple dates back to the time of the Mughal Emperor Akbar, who granted land for the construction of the temple to Guru Ram Das, the fourth Sikh Guru. The construction began in 1577 and was completed in 1604, with the installation of the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism. The fifth Guru, Guru Arjan Dev, oversaw the installation of the scripture and appointed Baba Buddha as the caretaker of the temple.

Cultural Activities at Golden Temple

The Golden Temple is not just a place of worship but also a center for cultural and religious activities. Devotees engage in prayers, meditations, and participate in the continuous recitation of the Guru Granth Sahib, the holy scripture of Sikhism. The temple is open 24/7, and visitors can witness the recitations and experience the soothing sounds of hymns at all times of the day.

The Langar, or community kitchen, is another key cultural aspect of the Golden Temple. Volunteers serve free meals to thousands of people every day, irrespective of their religion, caste, or background. This practice represents the Sikh principles of selfless service and equality.

Best Time to Visit

The Golden Temple can be visited throughout the year, though the temple is particularly crowded during the months of October to March, when tourists and pilgrims from around the world flock to Amritsar. Visiting during the Baisakhi Festival or the Gurpurab (the birthday of Guru Nanak) is especially significant, as the temple is beautifully decorated, and special prayers and processions are held.

How to Reach Golden Temple

By Air

The nearest airport to the Golden Temple is Sri Guru Ram Dass Jee International Airport in Amritsar, which is located about 15 km from the city center. The airport offers both domestic and international flights, connecting Amritsar to major cities in India and abroad.

By Train

Amritsar is well connected by rail to several major cities across India. The Amritsar Railway Station is about 2 km from the Golden Temple, and visitors can take a taxi or auto-rickshaw to reach the temple easily.

By Road

Amritsar is accessible by road from cities such as Delhi, Chandigarh, and Jammu. Buses, taxis, and private cars can be used to travel to Amritsar from these cities.
